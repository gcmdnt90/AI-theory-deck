Module 7

Security & risks

Three layers of failure: the model, the user, and the data.

Organise the module on three risk owners: model, user and data. This structure should stick even if they forget the individual examples.

model What the model does wrong

Hallucination

Fluent, confident, false.

Bias

Training-data skews carried into outputs.

Sycophancy

Agreeing with the user over being correct.

All three trace back to Module 2: the model optimises plausible text, not truth.

Sources: Ji et al. (2023); Perez et al. (2022); Sharma et al. (2023).

Connect causally to the engine: hallucination is sampling from a distribution with no truth oracle. Manageable, but not eliminable.

user What happens to us

Cognitive delegation

Offloading thinking can erode the skill being offloaded.

Fluency trap

Polished output feels more accurate than it is.

Echo chamber risk

Recommendation and social systems can amplify familiar beliefs and misinformation.

Sources: Risko & Gilbert (2016); Sparrow, Liu & Wegner (2011); Lee & See (2004); Lee et al. (2025); Del Vicario et al. (2016); Cinelli et al. (2021).

Most relevant slide for an academic audience worried about students. The risk is not only a wrong answer; it is slow atrophy of the capacity to tell.

data Where data leaks

Cloud vs local

Who can see prompts and uploaded data?

Prompt injection

Untrusted text becomes an instruction.

Secret exposure

RAG corpora and credentials become targets.

The dangerous jump is when untrusted text stops being data and starts steering an action.

Sources: Greshake et al. (2023); OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications.

Drive home that connectors widen the attack surface: the agent now acts on untrusted input. Prompt injection has no clean fix yet; design for least privilege.
