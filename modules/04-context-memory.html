Context & memory

The window, its limits, and how systems remember beyond it.

Core distinction: context is what is in the prompt right now; memory is what persists across turns or sessions. They are engineered differently.

The context window

Context is the model's immediate working memory: what is loaded for this call.

SystemRules and role instructions sit at the front of the working set.
History + RAGConversation and retrieved chunks compete for the same finite space.
Answer roomThe model also needs budget to produce the next tokens.
Bigger windows help, but they are not free.
Make tokens tangible: roughly three quarters of a word each. The window is working memory, wiped every call unless you re-supply it.

Bigger window, weaker middle

Models retrieve best from the start and end of long contexts.

The middle often becomes the quiet danger zone.

Source: Liu et al. (2023/24), Lost in the Middle, TACL.

Counter the "just use a huge window" reflex. Relevance curation beats dumping everything in.

RAG - retrieve, then generate

Same embeddings from Module 2 - now used as a search index.

Sources: Lewis et al. (2020); Karpukhin et al. (2020).

RAG is the single most useful pattern this audience will actually build: retrieve the few relevant chunks, not the whole library.
